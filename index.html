Beyond Vocal-Only: Music-Preserving Singing Voice Conversion via Mixture of Vocal and Music Representations

Abstract

Singing voice conversion (SVC) aims to transform a source singer’s voice into a target singer while preserving lyrical content and melody. However, existing SVC systems output only converted voice and ignore background music, limiting their applicability in real-world music production. In practice, users often wish to convert the singing voice directly from mixed audio while retaining the accompaniment to obtain a ready-to-release song. In this paper, we propose a singing voice conversion with preserved background music system which consists of vocal–music separation, feature extraction for both signals, an SVC model jointly conditioned on vocal and background music representations, and a universal vocoder. Furthermore, we introduce a data augmentation strategy to improve voice clarity and robustness. Both objective and subjective evaluations show that our framework performs well in both voice conversion and accompaniment signal preservation.
